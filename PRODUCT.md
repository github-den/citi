# Product

## Register

product

## Users
The primary users for the current product direction are LGU Admins, Barangay Admins, and Super Admins working inside the admin workspace.

LGU Admins use the platform to review feedback assigned to their office, verify reports, coordinate resolution work, transfer misrouted cases, and delegate barangay-level action when needed.

Barangay Admins use the platform to receive delegated feedback, update verification and resolution progress, and provide official responses for barangay-scoped issues.

Super Admins use the platform to oversee the entire system, manage admin accounts, review citizen reports, govern office and barangay references, and maintain accountability across the platform.

Citizen users remain an important secondary audience because the admin experience exists to respond clearly and responsibly to the feedback they submit.

## Product Purpose
CitiSense is a civic voice platform that helps local government teams turn citizen feedback into visible, accountable action.

The citizen-facing side enables residents to submit, track, and engage with civic concerns, suggestions, and compliments. The admin-facing side gives LGU, Barangay, and Super Admin roles a structured workspace for reviewing reports, coordinating responses, resolving issues, and maintaining trust in the platform.

Success looks like an admin experience that is fast to scan, easy to understand, and reliable under operational pressure, so public-sector teams can move from intake to action without confusion or unnecessary friction.

## Brand Personality
Modern, sleek, professional.

The product should feel credible, calm, and operationally mature. It should communicate competence and readiness without becoming cold, heavy, or bureaucratic.

Reference direction:
- shadcn/ui for clean system thinking and composable UI clarity
- V0 by Vercel for polished structure, spacing, and modern interface rhythm
- Supabase for product confidence, technical cleanliness, and restrained visual sharpness

## Anti-references
The product should not look playful, whimsical, overly trendy, or casually decorative.

Avoid visual directions that feel toy-like, loud, bubbly, gamified, or socially playful. The interface should not lean into “fun modern startup” energy when the user is trying to do serious civic operations work.

## Design Principles
1. Operational clarity first
Every admin screen should make the next responsible action obvious. Scanning, filtering, and status understanding matter more than ornamental styling.

2. Professional calm under pressure
The interface should feel stable and trustworthy even when the user is handling sensitive reports, account actions, or unresolved civic issues.

3. Role-shaped experiences
LGU Admins, Barangay Admins, and Super Admins should each see a workspace that reflects their actual responsibilities, scope, and decision-making needs.

4. Accountability should feel built in
Statuses, moderation actions, logs, and system controls should read as deliberate and auditable, not casual or ambiguous.

5. Modern polish without playfulness
The UI should feel current and refined, but never cute, flashy, or novelty-driven. Clean hierarchy and thoughtful restraint should carry the experience.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
The product should target WCAG AA accessibility.

Admin workflows should support strong contrast, clear hierarchy, keyboard-friendly controls where possible, readable status distinctions, and layouts that remain understandable under reduced visual or cognitive load.
